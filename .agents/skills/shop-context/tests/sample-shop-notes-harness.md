## Notes From Harness Manufacturing Engineer

- `Buzz Out` means continuity test.
- `E-Test` and `Electrical Test` are used interchangeably.
- `Lead Prep`, `Wire Prep`, `Cut / Strip`, and `Prep Leads` usually mean wire cutting and stripping.
- `Terminate` can mean crimp only or crimp plus solder depending on the PRT.
- `Sleeve`, `Heat Shrink`, and `Insulate Splices` are related but not always identical.
- `Process Leads` is a vague router term and must be interpreted from the PRT.
- Work center `3100` includes both continuity and hipot equipment, but not every harness gets hipot.
- Some older routers combine crimping and heat shrink into one operation.
- Work center `2100` is wire prep.
- Work center `2200` is termination / crimp.
- Work center `2300` is solder / sleeve / heat shrink.
- Work center `2400` is harness board assembly.
- Work center `4100` is final inspection.
