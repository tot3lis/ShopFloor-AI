## Process Overview
Post cure is an oven exposure after molding that can complete cure reactions, stabilize material properties, or reduce volatile byproducts depending on material system. In this shop, `OV-9 Blue M Post Cure Oven` supports `EL-SEAL-12` `045 Post Cure`.

## Core Principles
Post cure depends on material chemistry, time, temperature, airflow, load arrangement, part thickness, and acceptance requirements. Silicone and elastomer compounds can require specific profiles; public general sources cannot define the shop recipe.

## Equipment / Tooling / System Principles
An oven provides heated environment and, depending on model/options, may control temperature uniformity, airflow, alarms, and ramp/soak behavior. A cure oven process is only meaningful when tied to material spec and actual oven performance.

## Key Process Variables
Temperature, time, ramp/soak behavior, airflow, load density, part spacing, material chemistry, and oven calibration/uniformity all affect post-cure outcome.

## Process Window Concepts
The post-cure window must be established by material/process authority. Too little exposure may leave properties unstable or volatiles high; too much or too hot exposure may degrade parts.

## Materials, Parts, Geometry, And Environment Interactions
Thicker parts heat more slowly. Loads stacked densely may see uneven airflow. Elastomer chemistry determines whether post cure is required and what it accomplishes.

## Common Failure Modes
Possible issues include undercure, overcure/degradation, odor/volatile retention, dimensional change, surface condition changes, and lot variation from uneven loading.

## Failure Mechanisms
Thermal energy drives additional cure or volatile reduction. Uneven temperature or airflow creates nonuniform exposure. Material-specific chemistry determines benefits and risks.

## Practical Heuristics
Treat post cure as material-spec controlled. Ask for compound data, cure profile, oven record, and acceptance criteria before drawing conclusions.

## Troubleshooting Logic
Separate molding cure, post-cure oven exposure, deflash effects, and inspection method. Do not assign seal condition to the oven without records.

## Important Terminology
- Post cure: thermal processing after molding.
- Soak: hold at target temperature.
- Temperature uniformity: variation inside the oven work zone.
- Volatiles: low-molecular substances that may be reduced by post cure.
